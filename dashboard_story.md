
# Dashboard Story

## Executive Sales Dashboard (2024-2025) — Narrative Walkthrough

---

## Opening: The Big Picture

The Executive Sales Dashboard tells the story of a ₹217M business that is profitable (15.35% margin, ₹33.3M profit) but faces hidden challenges beneath the surface. While headline numbers look healthy, drilling into regions, categories, segments, and operations reveals specific areas where profit is being silently eroded.

---

## Act 1: Where Does the Money Come From?

### Regional Performance

The story begins with geography. **South region is the clear leader at ₹65M** — contributing nearly a third of all revenue. North follows at ₹55M, while East and West are tied at ₹49M each.

**The question this raises:** Why is South outperforming by ₹16M? Is it market size, sales team effectiveness, or product-market fit? And can we replicate that success elsewhere?

### Customer Segments

Revenue is remarkably balanced across segments — Home Office (₹74.5M), Consumer (₹71.9M), and Corporate (₹70.6M) each contribute roughly one-third. This diversification is a strength — no single segment failure would cripple the business.

**But there's a catch:** Home Office generates the most profit (₹11.6M) with the highest margin (15.57%), yet also has the highest return rate (5.67%). The segment that gives the most also takes back the most.

---

## Act 2: What Drives Profit?

### Category Profitability

Technology is the undisputed profit engine. Four Technology sub-categories (Copiers, Accessories, Phones, Machines) generate ₹28M+ in profit — **84% of total profit from just one category**.

Meanwhile, Office Supplies' five sub-categories combined produce only ₹1,705K — less than a quarter of what a single Technology product (Copiers ₹7,310K) generates alone.

**The insight:** This business is essentially a Technology company that also sells Furniture and Office Supplies. Strategic decisions should prioritize protecting and growing Technology margins.

---

## Act 3: What's Destroying Profit?

### The Discount Problem

The scatter plot reveals an uncomfortable truth: **discounts are destroying value**. With a correlation of r = -0.27, every percentage point of discount chips away at profitability.

Technology products — the highest-margin items — show the steepest decline. A Copier sold at full price might generate ₹100K+ profit, but at 30% discount, that same sale barely breaks even.

**The story here is clear:** The sales team is trading long-term profitability for short-term volume. Discount discipline is the single highest-leverage improvement available.

### The Return Rate Problem

The heat map exposes a specific vulnerability: **Furniture sold to Home Office customers has a 9.14% return rate** — meaning nearly 1 in 11 orders comes back.

This isn't random. Home Office customers likely buy furniture without seeing it in person, face delivery challenges in residential settings, and have higher aesthetic expectations for their personal workspace.

---

## Act 4: Operational Efficiency

### Shipping & Returns

The shipping analysis reveals a counterintuitive finding: **faster shipping correlates with fewer returns**. Same Day delivery achieves a 3.15% return rate versus 4.90% for Standard (3-4 days).

This suggests that delivery speed isn't just a customer satisfaction metric — it's a profit protection mechanism. Customers who receive items quickly may experience less buyer's remorse and are less likely to find alternatives during the waiting period.

---

## Closing: The Strategic Narrative

This dashboard tells the story of a business at a crossroads:

1. **Revenue is strong** (₹217M) but geographically concentrated in South
2. **Profit is healthy** (15.35%) but dangerously dependent on Technology
3. **Growth is balanced** across segments but return rates threaten margins
4. **Discounting is habitual** but demonstrably value-destructive
5. **Operations are functional** but shipping speed could be leveraged strategically

**The single most important action:** Implement discount controls. At r = -0.27 across 4,200 transactions, the data is unambiguous — discounts above 20% destroy more value than they create.

---

## Interactive Features

The dashboard includes 4 interactive filters (Region, Category, Customer Segment, Shipping Delay Bucket) that enable executives to:

- **Isolate a region** — see how South's category mix differs from East's
- **Focus on a category** — understand Technology's performance across all dimensions
- **Examine a segment** — deep-dive into Home Office's return problem
- **Filter by shipping speed** — compare Same Day vs Standard across all metrics

A **click-to-filter action** on the Regional Performance chart allows clicking any region bar to filter the entire dashboard to that region's data.
